⚠️
Food in Rooms

Some rooms require written confirmation that food is approved. When booking, flag whether food is planned. Don't ignore a follow-up — rooms can be pulled if food policies aren't confirmed.

Accessibility

Confirm the room has accessible entrance, seating, and restrooms. For Zoom/hybrid events, consider whether automatic captions are enabled. Flag any specific needs from attendees noted in the RSVP form.

3 Create an RSVP Form

Use Google Forms or Microsoft Forms. Check responses regularly leading up to the event to adjust food count, room-size risk, and dietary planning.

Standard Fields

Skip dietary fields if no food is provided. Always include the listserv checkbox.

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Role (student / faculty / staff / community)
  • Department / college
  • Food allergies / dietary needs (only if food is provided)
  • ☑ "Add me to the ERC listserv"
  • ☑ "Will you attend via Zoom?" (speaker/lecture events only)
✅
Using RSVP Responses
  • Check responses every 2–3 days in the week before the event
  • Use headcount to finalize catering quantities
  • Note dietary restrictions on the catering order by name
  • If Zoom RSVPs are high, confirm hybrid AV is set up correctly
  • Add listserv opt-ins to the ERC listserv at listserv.tamu.edu

Social Media & Photography

Photos and posts help build the ERC's visibility. Assign someone (usually GA) to take photos day-of.

  • Get verbal consent before photographing attendees — a general announcement at the start of the event is fine
  • Capture: welcome/check-in, room setup, speaker presenting, audience, food spread
  • Post recap within 2–3 days after the event (LinkedIn, Instagram) with: event name, speaker/topic, 1–2 photos, and a short description
  • Tag the speaker and any co-sponsors if appropriate
  • Save photos to the ERC shared drive for future use

Quantity & Dietary Guidelines
  • Order for roughly 80–90% of RSVPs to account for no-shows
  • Always include at least one vegetarian and one gluten-free option
  • Check RSVP form responses for specific dietary restrictions — note them on the order
  • Label dietary items on the table (small tent cards work well)
  • Pick-up is fine for small events (<10 people) but use delivery for anything larger
6 Speaker Coordination

Applies to EdTalk and In the Works events. Start speaker outreach as early as possible — 6+ weeks out is ideal.

Collect from the Speaker
  • Full name + title + institution/department
  • Short bio (2–4 sentences) for email announcements and the ERC website
  • Headshot (high-res PNG or JPG) for flyer and website
  • Talk title and abstract for the event blurb
  • Slide deck — request at least 3 days before the event; offer to load onto room computer in advance
  • AV needs — does the speaker bring a laptop? Any video/audio clips? Adapters needed?
  • Travel/reimbursement needs if the speaker is external
Pre-Event Check-in

For external speakers or first-time EdTalk presenters, do a brief check-in (5–10 min call or email) 1 week before to confirm:

  • Logistics: time, room, parking, what to bring
  • Format: how long for talk, when to take Q&A
  • Whether they want a moderator intro or will introduce themselves
  • Recording: confirm consent (yes/no/partial)
  • Zoom: if hybrid, will they present from the room or remotely?
Honorarium & Reimbursement
⚠️
Start This Early

Reimbursement and honorarium paperwork can take weeks. Confirm whether the speaker needs an honorarium or travel reimbursement during initial outreach — not the week of the event.

  • Submit any honorarium or reimbursement paperwork through the TLAC business office at the same time as the purchase request
  • Collect travel receipts from the speaker on the day of or within 1 week after
  • Keep a copy of all submitted paperwork
7 Day-of Guide
Parking

Include parking info in the 24-hour reminder email if the event is on central campus or at Gilchrist.

  • Central campus events — Gene Stallings Garage ↗ — validation passes available
  • Gilchrist events — Fan Field Parking Lots ↗
  • Passes: get from Andy or Kate, or request via Robin Burns through the ERC account
  • Bring passes to the event and be ready to direct attendees at check-in
Signage & Check-in
  • Print a welcome sign for the entrance to the room (event name, time, directions if needed)
  • Print directional signs for hallways if the room is hard to find in a large building
  • Set up a check-in table near the entrance with a sign-in sheet or name tag station
  • Name tags: pre-print for expected attendees if time allows; have blank ones + markers for walk-ins
  • Have a program or printed agenda available if the event warrants it
AV & Zoom

Arrive early and test everything before attendees show up.

Confirm the room has: projector/screen · HDMI/USB-C adapter · microphone (large rooms) · podium · tables for food and check-in

Bring (usually Kate): laptop charger · adapter · clicker · tape · markers · extra pens

💻
Hybrid / Zoom Checklist
  • Open the ERC standing Zoom link — do NOT create a new link
  • Test audio and video at least 30 min before start
  • Assign someone (GA) to monitor the Zoom chat and relay questions
  • Confirm recording consent with speaker before pressing Record
  • Enable Zoom captions if any remote attendees have accessibility needs
Roles & Setup

Arrive 30 minutes early. Assign roles before attendees arrive:

RoleResponsibilitiesUsually
Greeter / check-in Welcome people, hand out name tags, point to food and seating GA
Food setup Receive delivery, set up table, label dietary items, manage cleanup KB or GA
A/V & Zoom Slides, mic, Zoom monitoring, recording AK or GA
Photography Capture event for social media; get consent announcement at start GA
Runner Last-minute errands, extra supplies, anything unexpected GA
Food Logistics & Cleanup
  • Confirm delivery address includes: building name + room number + best entrance
  • Set food on a dedicated table away from the main seating/presentation area if possible
  • Label dietary items (tent cards: "Vegetarian", "Gluten-free", etc.)
  • Keep napkins, utensils, plates, and a trash bag at the food table
  • End of event: bag all trash, box or dispose of leftovers, return room to original layout
  • Stop Zoom recording, save and rename the file before closing out
Contingency Planning
🔄
If Something Goes Wrong
  • Speaker cancels (last-minute): Send a brief email to registered attendees ASAP with apologies and a reschedule date if known. Consider hosting an informal social instead.
  • Catering is late: Start the program on time; let attendees know food is coming. Contact the vendor immediately and have snacks as backup if possible.
  • AV fails: Have IT support contact ready (help@tamu.edu or your building contact). If projector is down, proceed without slides and have the speaker adapt.
  • Room not accessible / wrong setup: Contact facilities immediately. Have a list of backup rooms in mind when booking.
  • Campus emergency / weather: Follow TAMU emergency protocols. Send cancellation email to all RSVPs. Update the ERC website and social channels.
8 Wrap-Up

Complete these within 1–3 days after the event. Wrap-up tasks are also tracked in the Wrap-up category of each event in the Hub.

  • Send a thank-you email to the speaker (if applicable)
  • Send a post-event survey to attendees if feedback is useful
  • Post recap to LinkedIn and Instagram (photo + 2–3 sentence summary)
  • Upload recording to the ERC website or shared drive (if consent was given)
  • Add listserv opt-ins from the RSVP form to the ERC listserv
  • Record final headcount and any notes in the ERC tracking spreadsheet
  • Submit reimbursement forms for speaker travel or catering (within 30 days)
  • Save final event blurb, flyer, RSVP link, and photo folder location for next time
  • Mark all event tasks as Done in the Tasks page

Post-Event Feedback Survey

A short Google Form sent to attendees within 24 hours of the event is useful for improving future events. Suggested questions:

  • Overall, how would you rate this event? (1–5)
  • What worked well?
  • What could be improved?
  • Would you attend a similar event in the future?
  • Any other comments?

Keep it to 5 questions maximum — shorter surveys get better response rates.

Full Planning Timeline

Standard timeline for speaker events (EdTalk, ITW). Adjust for Happy Hours and internal events.

6+ weeks
  • Confirm speaker, date, and location
  • Submit event in the Hub to generate task list
  • Request bio, headshot, and talk title from speaker
  • Send preliminary hold to attendee calendar
  • Start honorarium/reimbursement paperwork if needed
4 weeks
  • Book room (with food flag)
  • Create RSVP form and share link
  • Send initial announcement to all channels
  • Post to ERC website
  • Submit to CEHD MarCom
  • Design and post flyer (building + digital signage)
2 weeks
  • Send reminder to all channels with RSVP link
  • Check RSVP count and finalize catering order
  • Confirm AV needs with speaker
  • Submit TLAC purchase request for food
  • Arrange any travel logistics for external speaker
1 week
  • Final reminder email to all channels
  • Pre-event check-in call or email with speaker
  • Confirm catering delivery time and address
  • Collect slide deck from speaker
  • Test Zoom link and audio if hybrid
  • Place catering order if not yet done
Day before
  • Load slides onto room computer or confirm speaker's laptop is ready
  • Confirm catering delivery time + address + ETA backup plan
  • Print name tags, sign-in sheets, welcome sign, directional signs
  • Send 24-hour reminder to all RSVPs: time + location + Zoom link + parking
  • Confirm parking passes are in hand
Day of
  • Arrive 30 min early — see Roles & Setup
  • Set up signage, check-in table, food, AV
  • Start Zoom, test audio, admit attendees from waiting room
  • Welcome attendees, photograph event (with consent)
  • Record headcount during or immediately after
1–3 days after
  • Send thank-you to speaker
  • Send post-event survey to attendees
  • Post social media recap
  • Upload recording and save assets
  • Submit reimbursements, update listserv
  • Mark all tasks Done in the Hub
