1 Event Basics
Key Points
  • Submit all new events via the Event Hub submission form — it goes to an approval queue.
  • Pick the right event type upfront — it drives the task template.
  • The approved event shows up on your dashboard automatically.
  • EdTalk Faculty research presentations. Zoom + in-person, ~30 attendees, catering + RSVP required.
  • In the Works Works-in-progress talks. Similar to EdTalk but lighter marketing.
  • Happy Hour Informal social. No RSVP, lighter catering, Gilchrist preferred.
  • Internal Team meetings, retreats, workshops. Minimal external marketing.
2 Reserve a Room
Key Points
  • Book MSC/Rudder at least 3 weeks out. Gilchrist can be shorter notice.
  • Confirm AV is included in your booking — do not assume.
  • Always send a calendar hold to the room contact right away.
MSC / Rudder — Robin Burns · robinburns@tamu.edu
Gilchrist — Andrea Trevino · andreatrevino@tamu.edu
  • What to request Room capacity, AV included, setup style, accessibility needs
  • Gilchrist Preferred for Happy Hours and smaller events. More flexible availability.
  • Confirmation Get written confirmation. Add room details to the event record in the hub.

Details below: Event: [Event Name] Date: [Date] Time: [Start] – [End] (including setup/teardown) Expected attendance: [#] AV needs: projector, screen, microphone[, Zoom capability if hybrid] Setup: [theater / rounds / classroom] Please let me know what's available. Thank you! [Your Name] Education Research Center, Texas A&M University
3 RSVP Form
Key Points
  • Copy the blank template form — never edit the original.
  • Share the copy link with Kate for review before publishing.
  • Use RSVP count to finalize catering order.
  1. 1 Go to the RSVP template folder in Google Drive. Duplicate the blank form.
  2. 2 Rename it with the event name and date.
  3. 3 Update event title, date, time, and location inside the form.
  4. 4 Share the edit link with Kate to review. Then publish and add to marketing materials.
4 Market the Event
Key Points
  • Submit to CEHD MarCom at least 2 weeks out — earlier is always better.
  • Newsletter blurbs go to Kate by Thursday noon for Friday send.
  • Flyer needs TAMU brand fonts/colors; export as PNG + PDF.
  • CEHD MarCom mycehd.tamu.edu/marcom — submit event for college calendar + email blast
  • Newsletter Submit blurb to Kate. Include RSVP link once form is live.
  • Flyer Use TAMU brand guidelines. Export PNG + PDF. Include speaker photo for EdTalks.
  • ERC Website Ask Andy to post once flyer is ready. See video walkthrough for process.
  • Slack Post in #erc-announcements once all materials are finalized.

Newsletter Blurb Template

[Event Name] | [Day, Month Date] at [Time] | [Location] [1–2 sentences describing the event and why it's worth attending. For EdTalks/In the Works: mention the speaker's name, title, and research topic. For Happy Hour: keep it casual and social.] [RSVP / more info: [link]] ← remove if no RSVP

CEHD MarCom Submission Checklist

  • Event title Official event name as it should appear publicly
  • Date + time Include timezone (CT)
  • Location Room name + building, or "Virtual via Zoom"
  • Description 2–3 sentences, plain language, no jargon
  • RSVP link Include if required, or note "Open to all, no RSVP needed"
  • Flyer Attach PNG + PDF
5 Food & Catering
Key Points
  • Slack Andy for 2FA before logging into EZCater — the account requires it.
  • Finalize headcount from RSVPs before placing order. Order at least 5 days out.
  • All food purchases go through a TLAC Purchase Request — see Andy for the process.
  • Login akwok@tamu.edu / tamuERC01! — Slack Andy for 2FA code first
  • Order timing 5+ days before event. Confirm delivery address and time.
  • Happy Hour No formal headcount needed. Budget ~$100. Appetizers / finger food only.
  • Dietary needs Check RSVP form responses. Note any allergies on the order.
  • Purchase request Complete TLAC form immediately after ordering. Attach receipt.
🛑 Never place a food order without a purchase request number. Get PO approval first.
6 Speaker Coordination
Key Points
  • Collect bio, headshot, and talk title as soon as speaker is confirmed.
  • Send calendar invite 1 week before the event with Zoom link + room location.
  • Request slides at least 2 days before so you can test AV ahead of time.
  • Bio + headshot Needed for flyer, website post, and intro slide. Ask early.
  • Calendar invite Use ERC standard template. Include Zoom info, room, parking, and day-of contact.
  • Slides Request 2 days out. Test on room AV before the event.
  • Day-of intro Kate or GA introduces the speaker using the ERC intro slide deck.
  • Honorarium If applicable — process via Concur through Andy. Get speaker's TAMU UIN or direct deposit info. Allow 2–3 weeks processing.

7 Signage & Check-in
Key Points
  • Grab the ERC event bag — it has name tags, markers, sign-in sheets, and tape.
  • Arrive 30 minutes early to set up and test AV.
  • Post directional signs from the building entrance to the room.
  • Event bag Name tags, markers, sign-in sheet, tape, printed agenda, table tents
  • Sign-in sheet Print from the templates folder. Collect name + department + email.
  • Signage Print directional arrows (in Drive). Tape at building entrance and hallway turns.
  • AV check Test clicker, laptop connection, Zoom audio/video before anyone arrives.
  • Accessibility Confirm accessible entrance route. Reserve seating near front for anyone who needs it.
8 Social Media & Photography
Key Points
  • Take photos of the speaker, audience, and food — upload to shared Drive folder same day.
  • Post to Instagram/X within 24 hours. Tag @TAMU_ERC.
  • Photo consent is implied for public events, but confirm if using individual photos externally.
  • Must-capture Speaker at podium, audience shot, food spread, any signage/branding
  • Upload ERC Events → [Event Name] → Photos folder in Google Drive
  • Social tags @TAMU_ERC (X/Twitter), @tamu_erc (Instagram), #TAMU #ERC
  • Recap Send 2–3 photos + blurb to CEHD MarCom within 1 week for newsletter/website use.
9 Contingency Planning
Key Points
  • Always have a Zoom backup ready — even for in-person events.
  • If room falls through within 1 week, contact Gilchrist first (most flexible).
  • Low RSVP count is not a reason to cancel — confirm with Kate first.
  • AV failure Have hotspot + personal laptop as backup. Know room IT contact.
  • Speaker cancels Notify Kate immediately. Check if rescheduling is possible before canceling.
  • Room issue Contact Gilchrist (Andrea Trevino) for last-minute space. Notify registrants ASAP.
  • Cancel comms Email registrants → Slack announcement → update ERC website. Kate approves message first.
10 Post-Event
Key Points
  • Send thank-you email to the speaker within 2 days — personal note preferred.
  • Log actual attendance in the event hub the same day.
  • Complete all remaining tasks in the hub and mark the event done.

Thank-You Email Template

Subject: Thank you — [Event Name] Hi [First Name], Thank you so much for [presenting at / joining us for] [Event Name]. We really enjoyed your [talk / presentation / time with us] — the audience was engaged and the feedback has been great. [Optional: add a specific detail or memorable moment.] We hope to have you back with the ERC again soon. Please don't hesitate to reach out if there is anything we can do for you. Warmly, [Your Name] Education Research Center, Texas A&M University
  • Attendance Enter actual headcount in the event record in the hub.
  • Photos Upload to Drive; send recap to MarCom within 1 week.
  • Leftover food Leave in the break room or offer to adjacent offices. Do not discard in bulk.
  • Signage Collect all printed signs. Return event bag stocked for next use.
11 Post-Event Feedback Survey
Key Points
  • Send the survey to attendees within 24 hours while it's still fresh.
  • Results go to Kate and Andy. Share highlights with the team in the monthly wrap-up.
  1. 1 Open the Feedback Survey template in Drive. Duplicate and rename for this event.
  2. 2 Update event name and date inside the form.
  3. 3 Email the survey link to the sign-in sheet list within 24 hours of the event.
  4. 4 Review responses at 1 week. Note attendance, satisfaction score, and open-ended themes.
